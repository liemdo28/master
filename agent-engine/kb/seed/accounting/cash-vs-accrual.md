# Cash vs Accrual Accounting — Choosing a Method and Tax Implications

## Overview

Two primary accounting methods govern when income and expenses are recognised:

- **Cash basis** — transactions are recorded when cash is received or paid
- **Accrual basis** — transactions are recorded when earned or incurred, regardless of cash timing

The choice affects reported income, taxes owed, and the accuracy of financial statements.

## Cash Basis Accounting

Under cash accounting, revenue is recognised when payment is received and expenses are recognised when payment is made.

**Example:** A consultant completes a project in December 2023 and invoices $5,000. The client pays in January 2024. Under cash basis, the revenue is reported in 2024 (when cash was received).

**Advantages:**
- Simple to maintain
- Directly reflects cash flow
- Tax is paid only when cash is received

**Disadvantages:**
- Does not match revenue with expenses in the same period
- Can distort income — a bad month may look good if prior invoices are collected
- Not acceptable for businesses with inventory (IRS rules)
- Cannot be used by C-corporations with gross receipts over $25 million (IRS §448)

**Who uses it:** Sole proprietors, small service businesses, freelancers.

## Accrual Basis Accounting

Under accrual accounting, revenue is recognised when earned (goods/services delivered) and expenses when incurred (obligation created), regardless of payment timing.

**Example:** Same consultant invoices $5,000 in December 2023 and receives payment in January 2024. Under accrual basis, revenue is reported in December 2023 (when earned).

**Advantages:**
- Accurately matches revenue with related expenses (matching principle)
- Provides a clearer picture of long-term profitability
- Required by GAAP for public companies and many regulated entities

**Disadvantages:**
- More complex — requires tracking receivables, payables, accruals
- Cash flow and reported income can diverge significantly
- You may owe taxes on income not yet received

**Who uses it:** Most medium and large businesses, all businesses with inventory (required), all C-corps over $25M gross receipts.

## Hybrid Method

The hybrid method uses accrual for purchases and sales of inventory and cash basis for other income and expenses. It is allowed by the IRS for certain businesses and simplifies inventory accounting while keeping other records simple.

## Key Concepts in Accrual Accounting

### Accounts Receivable
Money owed to the business for goods or services already delivered. It is an asset because it represents future cash inflows.

### Accounts Payable
Money the business owes for goods or services already received. It is a liability because it represents future cash outflows.

### Accrued Revenue
Revenue earned but not yet invoiced or received. Recorded as an asset (accrued receivable).

### Accrued Expense
Expense incurred but not yet paid or recorded. Recorded as a liability (accrued liability).

### Deferred Revenue (Unearned Revenue)
Cash received before the service is performed. It is a liability until the performance obligation is met.

**Example:** A SaaS company receives $12,000 annual subscription payment upfront. Each month, $1,000 is recognised as revenue, and the deferred revenue liability decreases by $1,000.

### Prepaid Expense
Cash paid for future benefit. It is an asset until used.

**Example:** Paying 6 months of rent ($6,000) upfront creates a prepaid rent asset. Each month, $1,000 is expensed and the asset decreases.

## Switching Methods

To change accounting methods, a business must file IRS Form 3115 (Application for Change in Accounting Method). A Section 481(a) adjustment is required to avoid duplicating income or deductions.

## IRS Rules Summary

| Situation | Required Method |
|-----------|----------------|
| Gross receipts ≤ $25M (3-year average), no inventory | Either allowed |
| Has inventory | Accrual for inventory items |
| C-corporation, gross receipts > $25M | Accrual required |
| Tax shelter | Accrual required |
| Publicly traded company (GAAP) | Accrual required |
